🎙️ EDH BROADCAST — GAME NIGHT DISPATCH

LADIES AND GENTLEMEN AND PLANESWALKERS OF EVERY COLOR IDENTITY — welcome to the absolutely unhinged recap of Game Night 2!


Tonight's broadcast is brought to you by Bryan "BPWyndon" himself, who apparently decided that winning one pod game wasn't enough, two games was merely a mulligan to six, and three consecutive victories was the only acceptable board state for a Friday evening. The man showed up with a bag full of Jund and Abzan commanders and proceeded to treat the entire table like his personal value engine.


We had a brand new player shuffle into the pod tonight — Noah sleeved up a Ghyrson Starn pinger deck and walked directly into the buzzsaw that is this meta. Respect. We also saw Adam debut three different commanders across the night (General Tazri, Amy Rose, and the 12th Doctor), because apparently brewing addiction is a color identity we're all just accepting at this point.


The night peaked with a Planechase finale so salty it registered a 5 on the sodium scale — the maximum setting, reserved for games that make you question your threat assessment AND your mana base. Eleven planes traversed. Chaos triggers everywhere. Bryan's Betor, Kin to All closed out the evening with authority while the rest of the pod planeswalked directly into emotional damage.


Four games. Five players. One very smug Szarel/Chucky/Betor pilot. Let's get into it.

One abrupt, hilarious, deeply unsatisfying (for everyone except Bryan) conclusion to a nearly two-hour attrition war. Chaos Warp resolved and ended the game. That's it. That's the death trigger.

Five players. Salt level: 3. Chucky has entered the command zone.

Bryan sleeved up the Secret Lair Kardur Doomscourge — the Rakdos goad commander wearing a Chucky skin — and proceeded to turn the entire pod into a forced combat hellscape. Kardur's ETB goad effect forced every creature to swing at someone who isn't Bryan, while he sat back behind a pillowfort of "you literally can't attack me" and watched the table eat itself alive.

But the finale — oh, the FINALE — was pure chef's kiss stack interaction. Bryan casts Rakdos Charm (mode: each creature deals 1 damage to its controller). A perfectly reasonable play. Adam, piloting the 12th Doctor spellslinger build, responds with Tempt with Mayhem and copies the Charm. Then copies it AGAIN. Suddenly every creature on every battlefield is pinging its controller multiple times over, and the whole table is just... taking lethal. Together. In a beautiful cascade of "damage to each player equal to the number of creatures they control" that Bryan set up from the moment he resolved Kardur.

This game ended with the entire table at zero and Bryan's Chucky standing in the wreckage as last man standing. Goad into Rakdos Charm into copied Rakdos Charm is now officially a win condition in this pod.

📝 "This game ended with Bryan casting Rakdos Charm, Adam copied it with Tempt with Mayhem, then we ended up killing everyone with the subsequent copies of Rakdos Charm."

MAXIMUM SALT. Five out of five on the sodium scale. The planar die is on the table. Shuffle your planar deck. Buckle up.

The pod decided to close out the night with Planechase EDH — because apparently four hours of regular Commander wasn't enough variance. We needed random global enchantment effects warping the board every few turns. We needed the planar die adding chaos rolls to every turn cycle. We needed eleven plane transitions in 87 minutes.

The multiverse tour hit some absolute bangers: Bloodhill Bastion (all creatures have haste and double strike on attack — a Rakdos player's Christmas morning), Asmoranomardicadaistinaculdacar's Kitchen (yes, that's a real plane name, and yes, Adam planeswalked them there on turn 5), Norn's Seedcore (Phyrexian infect nightmares), and a late-game Unleash the Flux phenomenon that shuffled hands and graveyards right when people thought they'd stabilized.

Jordan brought out Rakdos, Lord of Riots for the first time — the "I ping you, then I cast Eldrazi at steep discounts" commander. And on turn four, he demonstrated exactly why that card reads "steep discounts." Rakdos cost-reduced out a Void Winnower. Turn. Four. For anyone unfamiliar: Void Winnower says your opponents can't cast spells with even mana values and can't block with even-CMC creatures. The table could not literally even. For five turns, three other players sat there staring at hands full of uncastable cards while Jordan's 11/9 Eldrazi stared back at them. Boards froze. Salt climbed. The entire pod entered a hostage negotiation with a colorless mythic.

Jordan eventually relented and self-sac'd the Winnower — but by then the sodium levels were already at max and the damage to the table's mental state was done. Five turns of "I literally cannot play Magic: the Gathering" will do that to a pod.

Adam debuted the Jodah, the Unifier five-color legendary tribal build (the DCC deck — thematically correct, competitively aspirational). Waid ran Avatar Aang into the multiverse for the second time tonight. And Bryan? Bryan brought Betor, Kin to All — the Abzan grind machine that generates incremental advantage every turn until the table runs out of removal. While everyone else was recovering from the Void Winnower trauma, Betor was quietly accumulating value across plane after plane.

By the time they planeswalked to New New York (turn 10, 1:40 AM energy), the writing was on the wall. Bryan's Betor had ground out enough board presence and card advantage across eleven planes to close it out. Three wins in a row. The BP Wyndfall is real, and the pod is going to need a serious threat assessment adjustment for Game Night 3.

📝 Max salt (5/5). Planechase finale. 11 planes traversed across 87 minutes. Turn 4 Void Winnower locked the table out of even-CMC spells for 5 turns. Jordan self-sac'd it but the salt was already baked in. Bryan completes the three-peat.
